background

Hello and welcome to our Moffitt Library Makerspace Project! We are Batu, Dan, Natalie, Sunny, Jeffrey, and Zulaika— a team of multi-disciplinary students across various majors, design experiences, and backgrounds from a club called Invention Corps Berkeley. We were offered the opportunity to reimagine the Moffitt Makerspace thanks to our mentors Nicole Brown and Annalise Philips. The Moffitt Makerspace (MM) is centered around collaborative and open workspaces for the entire UC Berkeley community to make and create regardless of design experience. After initial observations, we felt that the Moffitt Makerspace, as a resourceful space on campus, lacked a clear identity and prescence at Cal. Futhermore, the Moffitt Makerspace is positioned for renovation as the Connected Center for Learning among Moffitt Library’s 1st to 3rd floors so this was the perfect time to begin considering changes for the future. Thus, we have shaped our work and research for the long-term structural and systematic redesign of the MM.

Our team was then posed with the question “how might we redesign this space to attract broader user groups and potential creatives?” We targeted the broad action items of rebranding, maximizing awareness, expanding current user groups, and redesigning for spatial efficiency. Given the current COVID-19 pandemic, we have readjusted those goals to create a guide tailored to the specific needs of the MM, including various user interview guides, design suggestions, marketing strategies and resources, and an assets kit for future implementation.

As creative communities and individuals become of more accepted in an age of innovation, we feel there is a need to support environments that foster creativity and the users of those spaces. Creating a welcoming, easily accessible, and comfortable workspace for all communities at Cal regardless of background or skill-level is imperative to our team. For many of us, the Moffitt Makerspace is positioned as an invaluable reource for all communities on campus to explore the infinite oppurtunities of innovation.

define

In the first week, we visited the Moffitt Makerspace in person individually. We noted down our initial thoughts on the space and tried to identify key features. We noted that there are some problematic qualities of the space that we could address.

After listing out our observations, we categorized the problems and defined three major pain points:

Based on common questions that students encounter and our personal experiences, we listed three main “How might we” questions.

1. How might we give the Moffitt Makerspace an identity?

2. How might we increase students’ engagement of the Makerspace and of the workshops?

3. How might we maintain a balance between facilitating the function of the space and ensuring the creative freedom?

Interview Key Findings

In our initial meetings, the team and mentors hosted a brainstorming session where we outlined important platform features and a basic user flow. This defined platform "skeleton" acted as a guiding pillar as the strategy and user interface teams moved forward in development.

We mapped out are ideas on a whiteboard so that we could easily visualize and elaborate on our ideas.

Key features we developed were:

  1. Establish student credibility
  2. Project preference forms
  3. Ways to discover projects
  4. A project filtering system

Our basic user flow:

general home→user log in→form to fill out basic info→home page while logged in→a way to look for projects→find project of interest→fill out specific questionnaire→get project→connect to project mentor

solution

UI Team

We can break the functionalities into 3 parts: searching for projects, creating an account, and organizing your profile and saved projects within your dashboard.

Searching for projects

Users can explore projects by filtering by area of study, time span, industry, etc. Users can save projects they like, which will then be stored in their dashboard.

via GIPHY

Creating an account

In order to save a project, user's must create an account. We collaborated with the strategy team to identify the key information we would want from students.

via GIPHY

Dashboard

Once a user creates an account, they gain a dashboard. The dashboard includes a profile to organize past experiences and skills, a place to see saved projects and choose which to apply to, and a place to store ongoing projects. Students can only apply to projects from their saved project list and are allowed to apply up to 5 projects at a time. We set these restrictions to differentiate our platform from other job sites and to enforce students to make more conscious choices when applying to projects. Our hope is to have students only apply to projects they feel passionate about.

next steps

The team had a wonderful time collaborating with Groupsource and its founders on this project. Because the project was at the early stages of development, we were able to play a large role in taking Groupsource from an idea to a prototyped platform. While performing user interviews, solidifying platform features and goals, and iterating through user interface prototypes; team members had the opportunities to grow individually through their contributions and make a lasting impact on a promising company. Team members learned Figma skills, business strategy techniques, successful interview structures and creative thinking. We look forward to seeing Groupsource interface with a new generation of learners.

Moving forwards, we hope to publish a Groupsource landing page and ultimately an official website using the Figma prototype we created. In tandem, we will also be coordinating the pilot program by solidifying the list of mentors and students that will participate. With development comes lots of user testing. In the beginning we plan on actively adapting the platform given feedback and testing.
