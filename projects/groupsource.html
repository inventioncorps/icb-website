background

Our team had the pleasure of partnering with Groupsource, a company and platform founded by James Hamilton and Riley Campbell that aims to connect inexperienced college students with professional opportunities where they can make meaningful contributions and develop their skillsets.

Both James and Riley served as our mentors throughout the design process. They helped us stay on course when defining deliverables, discussing business models, and prototyping the platform.

We set out to answer the question "How might we connect students with professional and relevant experiences to better prepare students for the work world." It is difficult for students in college to build a skillset and project portfolio in a field they are interested in without involvement in highly selective campus clubs or pre-established networking connections.

Inspired by organizations like Invention Corps that connect students with companies via semester long projects, one goal of Groupsource is to increase accessibility to opportunities that would prepare students for the workforce. We aimed to build a platform that connects students with organizations such as startups, non-profits, professors, etc that would benefit from student work. As an open-to-all platform, Groupsource would remove typical barriers to student involvement such as inexperience, limited campus organization selection, and competitiveness. We defined project goals that pushed us to explore the needs of our target population, the structure of our programs, and our ideal customer outcomes.

Business Process Flow Charts

Amongst all the previous factors and considerations, an integral component of the Strategy Team's goals was to ideate the Pilot Program experience, which was achieved through Business Process Flow Charts (BPFCs). The team initially split up creating four individual BPFCs before integrating all of them into one centralized BPFCs as shown below

The Above BPFCs runs on a project that lasts 10 weeks. In the "Pre-Project" stage, the three important aspects included:

  1. Project Parameter Definition - Project Leaders, based on responses to the project factors during their on-boarding, develop an initial plan and framework for the Curriculum not only achieve the project goals but ensure student's can achieve potential Learning Goals
  2. Project Preference Form - Students fill in the on-boarding Project Preference Form (PPF) and describe their desired Learning Goals
  3. Project Matching and Curriculum Development - Based on the PPF, groupsource will match students to their desired project and work with the Project Mentors to develop their frameworks into a full fledged Curriculum

As the project progresses, there are periodic weekly check-ins called Learning Goals Skill Checks which students will fill out. Based on this, Project Mentors and Groupsource will work together to adjust the curriculum on a dynamic basis as the timeline progresses.

Many of these aspects require hands on involvement from Groupsource, especially in the Pilot Program. However with scale, the nature of Groupsource's involvement will become more supervisory as an exemplar project timeline evolves.

To outline the basic flow, a user starts with the home page, then they can start looking for a project and save ones they like. From the saved projects, users choose which to apply to. Design Criteria

Logo

We made the header simple and bold, with two tones that can be adjusted accordingly to the background. We opted for a lowercase spelling to appear more casual and for students.

Colors

Our color choices stem from current UI color trends—purple is a very stable, gentle, yet mystic color, and we thought that would be perfect for a platform that runs on the creativity and success of its users.

Illustrations

The last design element we want to feature is our illustrations. We created a whole illustration pack, intended for use throughout the platform. While we didn't get to implement it in this version, hopefully they will be used as pieces for user onboarding process or homepage assets in the future!

solution

UI Team

We can break the functionalities into 3 parts: searching for projects, creating an account, and organizing your profile and saved projects within your dashboard.

Searching for projects

Users can explore projects by filtering by area of study, time span, industry, etc. Users can save projects they like, which will then be stored in their dashboard.

Creating an account

In order to save a project, user's must create an account. We collaborated with the strategy team to identify the key information we would want from students.

Dashboard

Once a user creates an account, they gain a dashboard. The dashboard includes a profile to organize past experiences and skills, a place to see saved projects and choose which to apply to, and a place to store ongoing projects. Students can only apply to projects from their saved project list and are allowed to apply up to 5 projects at a time. We set these restrictions to differentiate our platform from other job sites and to enforce students to make more conscious choices when applying to projects. Our hope is to have students only apply to projects they feel passionate about.
